THE HISTORY OF MEMES

“Much coin. Very chain. Such community.”


Dogecoin (DOGE) — born in 2013 from an internet joke, created by Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer, DOGE became the archetype of the memecoin era. What started as satire evolved into a cultural phenomenon and on-ramp for millions. Community, tipping, and a playful spirit powered it into the mainstream.


Origins: Shiba Inu “Doge” meme; early crypto culture; internet tipping economy.

The Dogecoin community cares about supporting each other, being kind, teaching people about cryptocurrency, fundraising, having fun, making memes, and being absurd.

This is nicely captured by Dogecoin’s unofficial tagline: Do Only Good Everyday.

“From meme to movement.”


Shiba Inu (SHIB) — launched in 2020 as a community-driven experiment in decentralized spontaneous growth. Dubbed the “DOGE killer,” SHIB built a sprawling ecosystem of tokens, NFTs, and DeFi utilities, proving memetics can bootstrap real network effects.


Origins: Anonymous founder Ryoshi; Ethereum-based token; community-first ethos.

Shiba Inu has proven that meme coins can evolve into meaningful projects.

With its Layer 2 network, DeFi ecosystem, and strong community, SHIB is more than just internet hype—it’s a project with growing utility and staying power.

“Attention is the asset.”

Pepe (PEPE) — 2023’s virality engine. Channeling one of the internet’s most enduring characters, PEPE crystallized how fast collective attention can mobilize liquidity. Pure memetics, minimal promises, maximum momentum.


Origins: Internet-native symbol; rapid exchange listings; social trading flashpoints.

Pepe is here to make memecoins great again.

Launched stealth with no presale, zero taxes, LP burnt and contract renounced, $PEPE is a coin for the people, forever. Fueled by pure memetic power, let $PEPE show you the way.

“The dog coin of Solana.”


Bonk (BONK) — Solana’s flagship memecoin that energized the ecosystem after a long winter. BONK turned community meme power into on-chain activity, integrations, and culture—proving that vibes can reignite a chain.


Origins: Launched on Solana; community airdrops; rapid ecosystem integrations and brand presence.

BONK helped catalyze builders, wallets, and apps on Solana, showing how memes can be utility-adjacent: tipping, brand collabs, and liquidity incentives tied to culture.

Whether treated as pure meme or as an ecosystem signal, BONK’s momentum reflects how communities coordinate attention into action.

“Cute shell. Cold execution.”


Pudgy Penguins (PENGU) — bridging NFTs and tokens, PENGU demonstrates how character IP, toys, and web culture can anchor crypto-native brands. A case study in persistence and community stewardship.


Origins: Cultural brand building; NFT-to-token crossover; mainstream storefront activations.

Igloo Inc. is the parent company behind the successful Pudgy Penguins project, with Luca Netz as its CEO.

A strong and engaging community is the backbone of the brand, with almost 140K members on Discord, 1.7 million Instagram followers, and over 540K followers on X
